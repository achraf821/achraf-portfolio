Who I Am

25 years old, and for as long as I can remember, I've been driven by an unwavering curiosity and a genuine desire to understand how things work, why they work, and how they could work better. That curiosity led me to computer science, and to one question that still fascinates me: how are all these devices able to do such seemingly magical things like communicating across the world, streaming content, powering entire platforms, all from chunks of silicon? How did we trick rocks into thinking? After my baccalaureate, the choice was natural. I wanted to understand it all from the ground up. That led me to INSA Toulouse, where I graduated as a Software Engineer.

My Approach

I approach every problem with a rational, almost mathematical mindset, breaking it down, questioning assumptions, and thinking critically before writing a single line of code. I believe a well-thought-out solution is always more valuable than a rushed one. I prioritize clean, maintainable code and constantly look for ways to optimize both performance and readability. Because at the end of the day, code is read far more often than it's written.
